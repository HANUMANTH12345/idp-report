\begin{spacing}{1.5}

The increasing adoption of renewable energy systems has significantly increased the need for efficient monitoring and maintenance of solar farms. Traditional inspection methods rely heavily on manual observation, which is time-consuming, labor-intensive, and often incapable of identifying faults at an early stage. The work presented in this report addresses these challenges through the development of a Visual Inspection of Solar Farm system that integrates drone-based imaging, image processing, and artificial intelligence for efficient infrastructure monitoring.

The aim of this work is to design and implement a system capable of detecting faults in solar panels through visual inspection and automated analysis. The proposed system utilizes high-resolution RGB images collected from solar installations and employs deep learning techniques for identifying defects such as dust accumulation, bird droppings, physical damage, electrical damage, snow coverage, and healthy panel conditions. A computer vision-based fault detection model was developed and trained on a dataset containing approximately 4000 annotated images belonging to six defect categories. 

The design and implementation of the system were validated through experimental testing and performance evaluation. Various testing conditions were considered to assess defect detection capability, classification performance, and operational efficiency. The trained model achieved a Precision of 70\%, Recall of 60\%, and a mean Average Precision (mAP@50) of 80\%, demonstrating its ability to effectively identify and classify solar panel defects across multiple categories. The inspection framework includes image acquisition, preprocessing, defect detection, fault classification, and automated report generation through a centralized monitoring dashboard.

The developed system demonstrates the effectiveness of combining artificial intelligence and computer vision for solar farm monitoring. The results indicate that the proposed solution can significantly reduce manual inspection effort while improving fault identification and maintenance planning. The integration of automated defect detection, classification, and reporting provides a practical, scalable, and cost-effective approach for enhancing the reliability, efficiency, and sustainability of modern solar energy infrastructure.
